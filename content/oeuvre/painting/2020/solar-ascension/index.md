One large triangle built from a hundred small ones — reds, pinks, golds — rising off a green field like heat. The triangle is the sun's shape when a child draws its rays, and the oldest mark on the rock faces. Painted in the first spring of the pandemic, when ascension was on everyone's mind one way or another.

Oil on linen, 34 by 34 inches.
