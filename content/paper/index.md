<p>Notes on various papers used by artists. </p><p>I source all my paper from <a href="https://www.takachpaper.com/">Takach Paper</a> in Albuquerque, New Mexico. The paper name links to the product on the Takach Paper website. Support local businesses and buy from Takach to keep printmaking alive and thriving! </p><h2 id="printmaking">Printmaking</h2><h3 id="copperplate">Copperplate </h3><figure class="kg-card kg-image-card"><img src="/paper/copperplate.jpeg" class="kg-image" alt loading="lazy" width="2000" height="1194"></figure><h3 id="coventry-rag">Coventry Rag </h3><p>This paper has an excellent feel to it. Very crisp, stiff and firm to the hand. I prefer the "smooth" finish to the "vellum" finish. The smooth finish feels stiffer and more pleasing. Both papers tear fabulously and leave very interesting deckle edges. </p><p>This paper takes watercolor ok - it has medium sizing I'll call it. But it is not the best.</p><figure class="kg-card kg-image-card kg-card-hascaption"><img src="/paper/coventry_rag.jpeg" class="kg-image" alt loading="lazy" width="2000" height="2000"><figcaption>Coventry Rag Smooth</figcaption></figure><figure class="kg-card kg-image-card"><img src="/paper/coventry-rag-2.jpeg" class="kg-image" alt loading="lazy" width="2000" height="2000"></figure><h3 id="domestic-etching"><a href="https://www.takachpaper.com/product/domestic-etching-paper/">Domestic Etching</a></h3><!--kg-card-begin: markdown--><ul>
<li>Very little sizing so it does not take watercolors or guache at all.</li>
<li>Soft fluffy touch to the paper</li>

</ul>
